# Figure Titles, Notes, Axes, And Legends

## Titles And Captions

- Titles should say what the figure shows, not what the author did.
- English: `Figure 1. Event-Study Estimates`.
- Chinese: `图1 事件研究估计结果`.
- Avoid mixing title styles within the same paper.

## Axes

- Label both axes with variables and units.
- If an axis is residualized, indexed, logged, standardized, or normalized, say so.
- Use readable tick intervals and avoid crowded rotated labels unless necessary.

## Legends

- Use legends only when direct labels are not practical.
- Keep legend names substantive: `Treated counties`, not `group1`.
- Put the legend where it does not cover data.

## Notes

Figure notes should state:

- sample or unit if not obvious;
- confidence interval or standard error convention;
- whether variables are residualized, weighted, binned, indexed, or normalized;
- source or construction detail needed to read the figure.

## What Not To Put In Notes

- Author workflow.
- Long variable construction text.
- Defensive explanation of why a visual was changed.
- Repetition of every regression control if the text already explains them.
