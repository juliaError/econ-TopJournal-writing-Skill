# Export Quality

## Preferred Formats

- Use vector PDF/SVG/EPS for line charts, event studies, maps, and diagrams when the journal workflow allows.
- Use high-resolution PNG only when Word compatibility or raster maps require it.
- Avoid JPEG for charts.

## Size And Resolution

- Export at the intended final aspect ratio.
- For Word-bound PNGs, prefer at least 300 dpi at final display size.
- Do not stretch figures inside Word or LaTeX after export.
- Keep figure widths consistent across the paper.

## Font And Line Checks

- Axis labels, tick labels, legend, and notes must remain readable at final size.
- Line widths should be thick enough for print but not heavy.
- Confidence intervals should be visible but visually secondary to the estimate.

## Final Artifact Check

Before delivery, inspect the actual PDF or DOCX:

- no clipping;
- no blurry text;
- no legend overlap;
- consistent caption style;
- consistent colors across related figures;
- readable grayscale version if printing is possible.

## Check

A successful export looks intentional in the final manuscript, not merely in the plotting software preview.
