# Mechanism Figures

## Rule

Mechanism figures should clarify a theory chain, institutional process, or empirical implication. They should not replace a mechanism test.

## Appropriate Uses

- Timeline of institutional steps.
- Diagram of actors, incentives, and behavioral responses.
- Flow from treatment to intermediate outcome to final outcome.
- Simple conceptual graph showing a comparative static.

## Design Rules

- Keep mechanisms linear unless the theory genuinely requires feedback.
- Label actors and actions, not vague boxes such as "mechanism."
- Use arrows only for directional logic that is argued in the text.
- Use one accent color for the focal mechanism and gray for context.

## What To Avoid

- Dense flowcharts that look like presentation slides.
- Decorative icons.
- Causal arrows unsupported by the empirical design.
- Multiple mechanisms in one crowded diagram.

## Check

After reading the figure, the reader should understand what observable implication the mechanism predicts.
