# Trend And Binscatter Figures

## Trend Figures

- Use for descriptive evolution, treatment/control comparison, or policy timing.
- Keep the number of lines small: one focal line, or two lines for treated/control comparison.
- Use consistent x-axis intervals and avoid over-labeling every year/month if it crowds the figure.
- Mark policy timing with a vertical line or subtle annotation.

## Binscatter Figures

- Use when the visual object is a conditional relationship after residualization or binning.
- State whether variables are raw, residualized, binned, weighted, or fitted.
- Use muted points and a clear fitted line.
- Avoid giving binscatter causal meaning unless the design supports it.

## Color Defaults

- Single focal trend: dark blue or black.
- Treated/control: dark blue for focal group and neutral gray for comparison.
- Multiple groups: use color plus line style, not color alone.

## Check

The figure should make one comparison easy. If it asks the reader to compare many lines, split into panels.
