# Heterogeneity Tables

## Rule

Heterogeneity belongs in the paper when it follows from theory, policy relevance, or identification concerns. It should not be a fishing expedition across every available subgroup.

## Recommended Designs

- Interaction design when the group dimension is central and coefficients are directly comparable.
- Split-sample design when interpretation is easier and sample sizes remain credible.
- Panel design when several theoretically related dimensions must be shown together.

## Table Structure

- Use a clear panel title for the heterogeneity dimension.
- Keep the focal treatment coefficient in the same row position across panels.
- Show group definitions in headers or notes, not only in variable names.
- Report observations by group.

## What To Avoid

- Many low-power subgroup columns in the main text.
- Group labels that are meaningful only in code.
- Comparing coefficients across groups without a test or clear interpretive caution.

## Check

If the heterogeneity result disappeared, would the paper's mechanism or policy interpretation become weaker? If not, move it to appendix.
