# Argument Spine

Use this file when the user asks to 理顺全文逻辑, restructure a paper, find the main thread, or decide what to emphasize.

## Core Spine

Every economics paper should be recoverable as one chain:

1. Question: what economic question is being answered?
2. Stakes: why does this question matter for economics, policy, or measurement?
3. Mechanism or hypothesis: what force should connect cause, behavior, institution, or equilibrium to the outcome?
4. Design or reasoning: what comparison, model, data, or institutional fact lets the paper answer the question?
5. Result: what is the central finding or proposition?
6. Interpretation: what does the result mean economically?
7. Contribution: what margin does the paper add relative to existing knowledge?
8. Boundary: where does the claim apply, and where should it not be pushed?

## Recommended Output

When auditing a draft, produce:

- one-sentence spine;
- missing links;
- misplaced material;
- repeated material;
- what to foreground;
- what to move to appendix or delete.

## Bad Pattern

The paper has a long motivation, then many data facts, then many regressions, but the reader cannot state the central comparison or mechanism.

## Better Pattern

The paper states the question early, names the comparison or model, previews the main result, and uses each later section to defend one link in the chain.

## Check

A reader should be able to summarize the paper in one sentence without reading the appendix.
