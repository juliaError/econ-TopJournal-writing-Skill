# Redundancy Linter

Use this file when the draft repeats motivation, literature, results, robustness, or policy claims.

## Delete Or Merge

Delete or merge material when it:

- restates the same motivation without adding a new economic object;
- repeats a literature claim already used for positioning;
- describes every table column instead of the central contrast;
- lists robustness checks that do not address a distinct threat;
- repeats contribution language without naming a new margin;
- explains the author's workflow rather than the reader-facing argument.

## Move To Appendix

Move material to appendix when it is credible but not central:

- routine robustness checks;
- exploratory heterogeneity;
- long institutional detail not needed for identification;
- supplementary descriptive facts;
- alternative specifications that do not change interpretation.

## Keep And Strengthen

Keep material in the main text when it:

- states the question;
- defines the identifying comparison or model logic;
- reports the central result;
- explains the mechanism;
- addresses the strongest threat to interpretation;
- clarifies scope or external validity.

## Check

For each paragraph, ask: if this paragraph disappeared, would the reader lose a necessary link in the argument? If not, delete, merge, or move it.
