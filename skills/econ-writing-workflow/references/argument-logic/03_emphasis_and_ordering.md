# Emphasis And Ordering

Use this file when deciding what should appear earlier, later, stronger, or weaker.

## Ordering Rule

Order the paper by reader need, not research chronology:

1. state the economic question;
2. give the design or reasoning path;
3. preview the main answer;
4. explain data, setting, or model only as needed;
5. present central evidence;
6. then present robustness, mechanisms, heterogeneity, and extensions.

## What To Emphasize

Emphasize material that is:

- necessary to understand the main result;
- a source of identification or theoretical leverage;
- a mechanism that explains why the result occurs;
- a contribution relative to a concrete literature, object, method, or setting;
- a boundary condition that prevents overclaiming.

## What To De-Emphasize

De-emphasize material that is:

- generic importance language;
- method branding without a substantive reason;
- descriptive background not used later;
- long literature lists without positioning;
- marginally related robustness or heterogeneity;
- policy claims not supported by the result.

## Original Pattern

Weak: `This issue is important and has received extensive attention. This paper conducts a series of empirical tests.`

Stronger: `This paper asks whether [economic force] changes [outcome] by comparing [units] before and after [source of variation]. The main estimate shows [direction and magnitude], consistent with [mechanism].`

## Check

The first two pages should reveal the question, design or reasoning, and central answer. If not, move the answer forward.
